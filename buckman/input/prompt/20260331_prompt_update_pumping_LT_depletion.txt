  Mission
  Correct the repo so it no longer asserts or tests that annual stream depletion must always be less than or equal to
annual pumping for the same year.

  This annual test is physically invalid for the Buckman system because depletion is a lagged response to the full
pumping history. Annual depletion can exceed annual pumping in low-pumping years due to residual effects from prior
pumping. A concrete historical example is 2019, when pumping was about 314 ac-ft but stream depletions were larger due
to lagged effects of earlier pumping.

  Use this revised conceptual wording as the report-level basis:

  “Cumulative stream depletion summed across all reaches does not exceed cumulative Buckman pumping. This conservation-
of-mass constraint is evaluated over the full simulation period (1972–present) rather than on an annual basis, because
the superposition model computes depletion as the integrated aquifer response to the entire pumping history;
consequently, annual depletion may exceed annual pumping in years of reduced well-field operation.”
